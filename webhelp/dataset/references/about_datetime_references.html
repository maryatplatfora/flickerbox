About Date and Time References

Slicing and dicing data by date is a very common reporting requirement. Platfora allows you to analyze date and time-based data at different granularities by automatically linking DATETIME fields to Platfora's built-in Date and Time dimension datasets.

Every DATETIME field in a dataset automatically references the built-in Date and Time dimension datasets. These datasets have a built-in hierarchy that allows you to explore dates at different granularities.

The source data for these datasets is added to the Hadoop file system when Platfora first starts up (in /platfora/system by default). If you have a different fiscal calendar for your business, you can either replace the built-in datasets or add additional ones and link your datasets to those instead. You cannot delete the default Date and Time references, however you can hide them if you do not need them.

The Date dataset has Gregorian calendar dates ranging from January 1, 1800 to December 31, 2300. Each date is broken down into the following columns:

Date Dimension Column Description
Date A single date in the format yyyy-MM-dd, for example 2013-02-14
Day_of_Month The day of the month from 1-31
Day_of_Year The day of the year from 1-366
Month Calendar month, for example January 2013
Month_Name The month name (January, February, etc.)
Month_Number The month number where January=1 and December=12
Quarter The quarter number with year (Q1 2013) where quarters start on January 1, April 1, July 1, or October 1.
Quarter_Name The quarter number without year (Q1) where quarters start on January 1, April 1, July 1, or October 1
Week The week number within the year where week 1 starts on the first Monday of the calendar year
Weekday The weekday name (Monday, Tuesday, etc.)
Weekday_Number The day of the week where Sunday is 1 and Saturday is 7
Work_Day One of two values; Weekend (Saturday, Sunday) or Weekday (Monday - Friday)
Year Calendar year, for example 2013

The Time dataset has each time of day down to the second granularity.
