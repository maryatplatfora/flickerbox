Understand Lens Disk Usage

The amount of space a lens uses on HDFS and the Platfora local disk depends on whether or not it shares data with another lens. When possible, Platfora shares lens files between lenses when those lenses share data. This allows Platfora to save disk space both on HDFS and Platfora.

Therefore, in some cases, unbuilding or deleting a lens will free up less disk space on HDFS or the Platfora local disk than the total lens size.

For example, if LensA is 400 MB total and 50 MB of the total is used by LensB, then unbuilding LensA frees up 350 MB of space. The other 50 MB of lens files remain on HDFS and the Platfora disk to use with LensB.

  • Determine Lens Size on HDFS
    The amount of space a lens uses on HDFS depends on whether or not it shares data with another lens. The System > Activities & Lenses page displays the total lens size including any files shared with other lenses.
  • Determine Lens Size on Platfora
    The amount of space a lens uses on the Platfora local disk depends on whether or not it shares data with another lens. The System > Resources page displays the size of the lens files stored on the Platfora local disk that are unique to each lens. The lens size does not include files shared with other lenses. Complete these steps to view view how much space will become available on Platfora when you unbuild or delete a lens.
