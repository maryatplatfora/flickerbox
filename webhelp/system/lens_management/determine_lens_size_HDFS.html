Determine Lens Size on HDFS

The amount of space a lens uses on HDFS depends on whether or not it shares data with another lens. The System > Activities & Lenses page displays the total lens size including any files shared with other lenses.

When possible, Platfora reuses lens files between multiple lenses whenever they share the same source datasets and fields. This allows Platfora to save disk space in both HDFS and Platfora. In some cases, unbuilding or deleting a lens will free up less disk space than is reported by the total lens size.

You can view how much actual disk space will be reclaimed on HDFS if you unbuild or delete a lens, accounting for the lens files that are shared between lenses. Disk space will only be reclaimed for lens files that are unique to the selected lens, not for any shared lens files.

  1. Go to the System page.
  2. Go to the Activities & Lenses tab.
  3. In the Lenses section, click the check box for the lenses you want to determine its size.
    Note: You can select multiple lenses to determine the total size on HDFS taken up by the selected lenses.
  4. Click Manage Selected.
  5. Select Unbuild, and click OK in the Manage Selected dialog.
  6. In the Confirm Dialog dialog, read the amount of space on HDFS that the unbuild process will free up.
    The displayed size will be less than the sum of the lens sizes as listed on the System > Activities & Lenses page if the selected lenses share data with other lenses.
  7. Click Cancel.
